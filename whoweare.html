About the Leadership

Meet our Board of Directors members and our Producers for the 2014-2015 school year!

Board of Directors

Holly Dayton

Holly is a sophomore majoring in History who's passionate about theater, corgis, social dance, and all things culinary. She's been involved in theater since her proud lower school performance as the bull in the nativity play. While at Stanford, she's been an Assistant Stage Manager for OWOA 2013 and acted in the ensemble of Les Misérables in the spring of 2014. Holly is also a member of Chi Alpha Student Ministries.

Liz Knarr

Liz is a junior double majoring in Political Science and Theater and Performance Studies. She has worked on a number of Ram's Head shows, including The Original Winter One Acts, Les Misérables, and Next to Normal, primarily as a director and as a stage manager. She has also enjoyed working with other theater groups on campus, including AATP, Stanford Theater Lab, and the TAPS Department. Elizabeth is so excited to be a member of the Ram's Head board this year!

Louis Lagalante

Louis is a senior pursuing a double major in psychology and music. His love of theater began in sixth grade, when he was cast as Mr. McAffee in Bye Bye Birdie. Since then, he has been an actor, pit musician, composer, production staff, and enthusiastic audience member. He is really looking forward to being on the Ram's Head board this year, and can't wait to see the great work that the 2014-2015 season will bring!

Matt Lathrop

Matt is a junior studying computer science and theater. At Stanford, he has previously worked as lighting designer for Les Misérables, Gaieties, and War of the Worlds, and was the associate lighting designer on My Fair Lady, Oxford Companions, and Moby Dick - Rehearsed. At Stratford Playhouse, he worked as a lighting designer, technical director, and stage manager on numerous productions and musicals.

Julia Starr

Julia is a junior studying the intersection of information technology, media/art, and society. In the theater world, she is primarily a playwright and producer. Her play, "Rails," was produced in the Original Winter One Acts 2013. Additionally, her first full-length play, "Crazy Runs in the Family" received its professional premier this summer at the Highwood Theatre in the DC area. She also produced the Original Winter One Acts 2014. To learn more about Julia, check out her website.

Producers

Keegan Livermore

Keegan is a physics studying producer who loves the little details. Raised in Portland, OR, Keegan partook in the Structural Liberal Education (SLE) program plays, opening his eyes to the opportunities on campus. Starting with his involvement with the Original Winter One Acts (OWOA) in 2014, he turned his sights upon the wonderful Cardinal tradition of celebrating the Farm on stage before the annual Big Game. For more information on how to get involved in the first Ram's production of the year, contact him at keegan (at) stanford (dot) edu.

Alexander Kucy

Kucy is a sophomore majoring in Electrical Engineering - but don't let that deceive you. He regularly crashes (or, as it is politely called, audits) classes from all across the humanities departments. Since arriving at Stanford he has been writer-director of the SLE Players' 're-invented' Lysistrata and The Misanthrope and has been producer for the FREEKS' Almost, Maine and Circle Mirror Transformation. He is now the producer of OWOA '15.

James Sherwood

James is a sophomore majoring in TAPS and Economics. His past theatre experience at Stanford include with OWOA 2014 and Next to Normal (Lighting Designer); Gaieties 2013 and Les Misérables (ALD) (with Ram's Head) as well as Violet the Musical (Assistant Producer) (with At The Fountain Theatricals). He is really excited to be producing Hairspray, and is looking forward to working with as many Ram's Head members, both new and old, as possible on the show!

Executive Producer

Nisha Masharani

Nisha is a senior majoring in computer science. She loves student theater and has worked with several theater groups on campus in a variety of roles. She has worked on the technical side of several Ram's Head shows, including (but not limited to): City of Angels, The Last Five Years, Gaieties 2012 and 2013, Spring Awakening, Les Misérables, and Next to Normal. She is excited to work with this year's fabulous Board of Directors, Producers, and Executive Staff on Ram's Head's 103rd season! To learn more about Nisha, visit her at stanford.edu/~nisham.
