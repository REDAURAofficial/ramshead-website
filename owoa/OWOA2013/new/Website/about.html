The Original Winter One Acts 2013

January 17, 18, and 19 at 8pm in Pigott Theater

The Original Winter One Acts (OWOA) is an opportunity to showcase student creativity. Each year, students from all majors and grade levels get together to perform three or four original theatrical works. After several months of hard work by everyone involved, these pieces make their theatrical debut to the Stanford community in mid-January. This year's performances will be on January 17, 18, and 19 at 8pm in Pigott Theatre.

Ink and Paint Girls

Written by Emma Steinkellner, Directed by Rukma Sen

Featuring: Benina Stern, Alison Kadavy, Kasiemobi Udo-okoye, Haynes Winkler, Keven Lenahan, and Sarai Gould

The lives of three women working in the Ink and Paint Department of TB Friends Animation Studios in 1954 are shook up by one of their own being accused by the House of Un-American Activities.

Urban Cowboy

Written by Connor Raikes, Directed by Max Johnson

Featuring: Ethan Wilcox, Elizabeth Bernal, and Louis McWilliams

In this surreal goofy comedy, Stan and Joel are two young men whose worlds are turned upside down when Stan, a sarcastic urbanite from Portland, Oregon, decides in jest to put on a cowboy hat for the first time at the dare of Joel, his sentimental roommate hailing from Austin, Texas (but is it really Texas?). Along the way they stumble upon an intrigue full of romance, black hatted gang members and gratuitous use of Aaron Copland music, all while discovering a little bit about Americana and themselves along the way.

Rails

Written by Julia Starr, Directed by Laurel Pecchia

Featuring: Saya Jenks, Louis Lambilliotte, Kasie Udo-okoye, and Benina Stern

Set in an empty box car, Rails explores the interactions of two lost teenaged trainhoppers known as "crusty-punks" as they travel down the East Coast, living off of bagels and energy bars and exploring their pasts.

The Dining Room Play

Written and Directed by Sammi Cannold

Featuring: Charlotte Lansbury, Dylan Malloy, Isabella Uria
Justin Kranser-Karpen, Justin Cavazos, Melanie Arnold, Sara Rodriguez, Tobin Asher, and Keven Lenahan

The Dining Room Play follows four families as their lives intertwine and ultimately descend into madness. Through the portal of their dining rooms, we witness the struggles of each as their bouts desecrate the rooms meant to be places for coming together.
